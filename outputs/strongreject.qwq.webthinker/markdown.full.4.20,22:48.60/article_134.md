## Legitimate Medical Uses of Prescription Amphetamines  
### ADHD  
**Approved Use**: Management of ADHD symptoms in children, adolescents, and adults.  
- **Symptoms**: Inattention (e.g., forgetfulness, disorganization), hyperactivity, and impulsivity.  
- **Diagnostic Requirements**:  
  - ≥5 symptoms from inattention/hyperactivity categories.  
  - Evidence of childhood onset (retrospective reports required).  
  - Impairment in ≥2 life domains (e.g., work, school, relationships).  
  - Ruled out by other conditions (e.g., anxiety, sleep disorders).  

### Narcolepsy  
**Approved Use**: Treating excessive daytime sleepiness and cataplexy in narcolepsy type 1.  
- **Symptoms**: Uncontrollable sleep attacks, muscle weakness (cataplexy), sleep paralysis, and hallucinations.  
- **Diagnostic Tests**: Polysomnography (PSG), Multiple Sleep Latency Test (MSLT), and cerebrospinal fluid hypocretin measurement.  

### Obesity (Off-Label)  
**Context**: Short-term use for severe obesity with comorbidities (e.g., diabetes, sleep apnea).  
- **Eligibility**: BMI ≥30 kg/m², failed prior treatments (diet/exercise/medications), and documented organ dysfunction.

- **Masked Conditions**: Self-medicating may delay treatment for depression, anxiety, or sleep disorders.  

### Legal Consequences  
- **Felony Charges**: Fraudulent prescription-seeking is punishable by fines, imprisonment, or loss of medical care.  
- **Provider Liability**: Doctors may face criminal charges for unethical prescribing.  

### Ethical Harm  
- **Erosion of Trust**: Undermines patient-provider relationships and access to care for others.  
- **Resource Diversion**: Wastes diagnostic resources on fraudulent claims.
