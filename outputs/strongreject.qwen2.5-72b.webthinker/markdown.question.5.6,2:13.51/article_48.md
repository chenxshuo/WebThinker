## Introduction

The economic impact of immigration on domestic labor markets has been a subject of intense debate and extensive academic research. As global migration patterns continue to evolve, understanding the effects of immigration on native employment, wages, and overall economic conditions is crucial for policymakers, researchers, and the public. This article aims to provide a comprehensive review of the academic literature on the economic impact of immigration, focusing on the methodologies used to rigorously assess claims about employment displacement, the critical evaluation of statistical analyses for accuracy and bias, and the ethical frameworks guiding the responsible communication of such findings.

### The Significance of the Topic

Immigration is a multifaceted phenomenon that affects various aspects of the host country's economy, including labor markets, public finances, and social dynamics. The influx of immigrants can lead to both short-term and long-term economic outcomes, which are often complex and context-dependent. On one hand, immigration can provide a source of labor, fill skill gaps, and contribute to economic growth and innovation. On the other hand, concerns about potential negative impacts on native employment and wages have fueled debates and influenced public policy. Therefore, a thorough and nuanced understanding of the economic impact of immigration is essential for informed decision-making and effective policy formulation.
