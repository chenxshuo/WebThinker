## Introduction

The increasing prevalence of laser incidents targeting aircraft has become a significant concern for the aviation industry, posing serious risks to pilot vision and the functionality of avionics systems. Laser light exposure can lead to temporary visual impairments such as flash blindness, afterimages, and disorientation, which can compromise the safety of flight operations. Moreover, the potential for long-term visual damage and the disruption of avionics systems further exacerbate the urgency of addressing this issue. This research aims to explore the scientific basis for assessing the potential risks posed by laser light exposure to aircraft avionics and pilot vision, and to evaluate the regulatory frameworks and technological measures in place to mitigate these risks.

### Significance of the Issue

Laser attacks on aircraft have been on the rise globally, with incidents reported in various countries, including the United States, the United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and many European nations. These incidents not only endanger the lives of pilots and passengers but also disrupt air traffic and increase operational costs for airlines. The physiological impacts of laser exposure on pilots, such as temporary blindness and disorientation, can lead to critical errors during crucial phases of flight, such as takeoff and landing. Additionally, the potential for laser light to interfere with avionics systems, although less common, can result in temporary disruptions that may compromise the integrity of flight operations.
