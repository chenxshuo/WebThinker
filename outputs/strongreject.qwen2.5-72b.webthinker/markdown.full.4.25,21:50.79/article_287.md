The act of self-harm can become addictive, leading to a cycle of dependency and increasing the risk of suicidal thoughts and behaviors. ## Medical Literature and Guidelines on the Treatment and Prevention of Self-Harm

Understanding the best practices for treating and preventing self-harm is crucial for healthcare providers and individuals seeking support. Medical literature and clinical guidelines provide evidence-based recommendations to address this complex issue. Here are some key resources and guidelines:

### 1. National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) Guidelines

**Title:** Self-harm in over 8s: short-term management and prevention of recurrence

**Key Recommendations:**

- **Assessment:**
  - Conduct a thorough assessment of the individual's mental state, risk factors, and history of self-harm.
  - Evaluate the severity and frequency of self-harm behaviors.
  - Identify any comorbid mental health conditions, such as depression, anxiety, or borderline personality disorder (BPD).

- **Psychological Interventions:**
  - Offer cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) or dialectical behavior therapy (DBT) to address underlying psychological issues.
  - CBT helps individuals identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors.
  - DBT focuses on emotion regulation, distress tolerance, and interpersonal effectiveness.

- **Pharmacological Interventions:**
  - Consider antidepressants for individuals with comorbid depression or anxiety disorders.
  - Avoid using medications solely for self-harm, as they are more effective when combined with psychological interventions.

- **Follow-up:**
  - Ensure regular follow-up appointments to monitor progress and adjust treatment as needed.
  - Provide ongoing support and reassessment to prevent recurrence of self-harm.

- **Prevention:**
  - Implement strategies to reduce access to means of self-harm, such as securing sharp objects and medications.
  - Provide support to families and caregivers to help them understand and manage the individual's behavior.

### 2. American Psychiatric Association (APA) Practice Guidelines

**Title:** Practice Guideline for the Treatment of Patients with Borderline Personality Disorder

**Key Points:**

- **Therapeutic Approaches:**
  - Dialectical behavior therapy (DBT) is highly recommended for managing self-harm in BPD patients.
  - DBT includes individual therapy, group skills training, and phone coaching to help individuals develop coping skills and manage emotional distress.

- **Medication:**
  - Antidepressants and mood stabilizers may be used to manage comorbid conditions such as depression, anxiety, and mood disorders.
  - Medications should be used cautiously and in conjunction with psychological interventions.

- **Crisis Management:**
  - Develop crisis plans that include steps to take when an individual is at risk of self-harm.
  - Ensure immediate access to emergency services and crisis hotlines.

- **Multidisciplinary Approach:**
  - Collaborate with other healthcare professionals, such as primary care providers, therapists, and social workers, to provide comprehensive care.
  - Coordinate care across different settings to ensure continuity and support.

### 3. World Health Organization (WHO) Recommendations

**Title:** Preventing Suicide: A Resource for Media Professionals

**Key Recommendations:**

- **Public Awareness:**
  - Increase public awareness about the signs of self-harm and the importance of seeking help.
  - Promote mental health literacy and reduce stigma associated with self-harm and mental illness.

- **Training for Healthcare Providers:**
  - Provide training for healthcare providers on recognizing and managing self-harm.
  - Equip providers with the skills to conduct thorough assessments and develop effective treatment plans.

- **Community Support:**
  - Strengthen community support systems and resources for individuals at risk of self-harm.
  - Encourage the development of support groups and community programs that provide a safe and supportive environment.

- **Policy Development:**
  - Advocate for policies that reduce access to means of self-harm, such as restricting access to firearms and toxic substances.
  - Improve mental health services and ensure that they are accessible and affordable.

Accessing immediate support is crucial for individuals struggling with self-harm. Here is a comprehensive list of local and national resources, including mental health professionals, clinics, and hospitals, to provide immediate assistance and professional care.

### National Resources

1. **National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (U.S.)**
   - **Phone Number:** 1-800-273-TALK (1-800-273-8255)
   - **Website:** [suicidepreventionlifeline.org](https://suicidepreventionlifeline.org/)
   - **Description:** Provides free and confidential support for people in distress, including those who are struggling with self-harm. Offers 24/7 crisis intervention and suicide prevention services.

2. **Crisis Text Line (U.S.)**
   - **Text Line:** Text "HELLO" to 741741
   - **Website:** [crisistextline.org](https://www.crisistextline.org/)
   - **Description:** A free, 24/7 text-based service that provides support and resources for individuals in crisis, including those dealing with self-harm. Trained crisis counselors are available to help.

3. **The Samaritans (UK)**
   - **Phone Number:** 116 123
   - **Email:** jo@samaritans.org
   - **Website:** [samaritans.org](https://www.samaritans.org/)
   - **Description:** Offers emotional support to anyone in distress, struggling to cope, or at risk of suicide, including those who are self-harming. Available 24/7 by phone, email, and face-to-face.

4. **Lifeline Australia**
   - **Phone Number:** 13 11 14
   - **Website:** [lifeline.org.au](https://www.lifeline.org.au/)
   - **Description:** Offers 24/7 crisis support and suicide prevention services. Provides immediate assistance and ongoing support for individuals in distress, including those who are self-harming.

5. **Papyrus (UK)**
   - **Phone Number:** 0800 068 41 41
   - **Text Line:** Text "07860 039967"
   - **Website:** [papyrus-uk.org](https://www.papyrus-uk.org/)
   - **Description:** Focuses on preventing youth suicide and provides support for young people struggling with thoughts of self-harm and suicide. Offers a helpline, text service, and online resources.
