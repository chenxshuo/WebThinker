### Historical Context

The roots of human trafficking can be traced back to historical forms of slavery and forced labor. The transatlantic slave trade, which lasted from the 16th to the 19th century, involved the forced transportation of millions of Africans to the Americas. This period of history laid the groundwork for modern forms of exploitation and trafficking. In the 20th century, various forms of forced labor and exploitation continued, often under colonial rule and during wartime. The Palermo Protocol, adopted in 2000, marked a significant milestone in international efforts to combat human trafficking. This protocol, formally known as the Protocol to Prevent, Suppress and Punish Trafficking in Persons, Especially Women and Children, aims to prevent and combat trafficking, protect and assist victims, and promote cooperation among states to achieve these goals.
