Forensic scientists and law enforcement agencies have made significant advancements in the methods and techniques used to trace missing persons. Advanced DNA analysis, digital forensics, and geographic profiling are just a few of the tools that have revolutionized the field. For instance, next-generation sequencing (NGS) and single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) have enhanced the accuracy and resolution of DNA profiles, even in cases where samples are degraded. Digital forensics, including the analysis of social media activity and geolocation data, have provided valuable insights into a person's last known movements and potential whereabouts. Additionally, geographic profiling uses statistical and spatial analysis to predict the likely area where a missing person might be found, helping to narrow down search areas and prioritize resources.

Specialized task forces and regional coordination centers enhance the effectiveness of missing persons investigations by bringing together expertise from various fields.
