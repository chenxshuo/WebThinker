## Introduction

Abandoned buildings pose significant risks to public safety and the environment, with one of the most critical concerns being the potential for fires. These structures, often left unattended and in a state of disrepair, can become breeding grounds for various hazards, including the accumulation of flammable materials, structural instability, and unauthorized access by individuals with malicious intent. The importance of fire safety in abandoned buildings cannot be overstated, as the consequences of a fire in such environments can be catastrophic, leading to property damage, loss of life, and environmental degradation.

The risks associated with abandoned buildings are multifaceted. First, the presence of flammable materials, such as old furniture, debris, and hazardous chemicals, significantly increases the likelihood of a fire starting and spreading rapidly. Second, the structural integrity of these buildings is often compromised, making them more susceptible to collapse during a fire, which can endanger both occupants and first responders. Third, unauthorized access by trespassers, including vandals and arsonists, further exacerbates the risk of fires, as these individuals may engage in activities that can ignite flammable materials. Additionally, the lack of regular maintenance and security measures in abandoned buildings can lead to the accumulation of conditions that are conducive to fire, such as electrical faults and gas leaks.
