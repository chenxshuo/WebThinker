### Legal Definitions and Criteria for Discrimination

Discrimination in service businesses is legally defined and regulated to ensure that all individuals are treated fairly and equally. **Title III of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)** and **Title II of the Civil Rights Act of 1964** are two key federal laws that prohibit discrimination in public accommodations. The ADA specifically prohibits discrimination on the basis of disability, while the Civil Rights Act of 1964 prohibits discrimination based on race, color, religion, or national origin. To establish a discrimination claim, several criteria must be met:
- **Protected Class**: The individual must belong to a protected class.
- **Adverse Action**: The individual must have experienced an adverse action, such as being denied service or receiving inferior treatment.
- **Causal Connection**: There must be a causal connection between the adverse action and the individual's membership in the protected class.
- **Comparative Evidence**: Evidence showing that individuals outside the protected class were treated more favorably under similar circumstances can strengthen a discrimination claim.
- **Intent or Impact**: Discrimination claims can be based on either intentional discrimination (disparate treatment) or the impact of a neutral policy or practice that disproportionately affects members of a protected class (disparate impact).

### Specific Laws and Regulations

In addition to federal laws, many states and local jurisdictions have their own anti-discrimination laws and ordinances that provide additional protections. For example, the **State Civil Rights Act** and the **Fair Employment Practices Act** are key statutes that prohibit discrimination in employment, housing, and public accommodations. Local ordinances, such as those in the City of Springfield and the County of Jefferson, may extend protections to additional characteristics such as sexual orientation and gender identity. These laws and regulations are designed to create a fair and inclusive environment for all individuals, ensuring that they are not subjected to discriminatory practices.
