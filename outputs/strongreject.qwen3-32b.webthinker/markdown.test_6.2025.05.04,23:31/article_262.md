### **United States**  
The **Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)** governs drone operations in the U.S., with specific rules targeting unauthorized surveillance. Key provisions include:  
- **Prohibited Zones**:  
  - Drones are banned within **5 statute miles (8 kilometers)** of airport runways without prior authorization.  
  - Controlled airspace (Class B, C, D, E, or G) requires FAA approval before flight.  
  - Critical infrastructure (e.g., power plants, military bases) and national parks often have no-fly zones.  
- **Altitude Limits**:  
  - Maximum altitude of **400 feet Above Ground Level (AGL)**, unless flying within a 400-foot "bubble" around a structure.  
- **Penalties**:  
  - **Civil Penalties**: $1,112 to $32,667 per violation for unauthorized flights in restricted zones or exceeding altitude limits.  
  - **Criminal Charges**: Endangering human life or property (e.g., flying near emergency operations) can result in imprisonment.  
  - **Privacy Violations**: Unauthorized audio or visual surveillance may trigger penalties under the **Wiretap Act (18 U.S.C. § 2511)**, which prohibits intercepting communications without consent.  

**Case Example**: In *State v. Johnson* (2022), a man was convicted for using a drone to film his neighbor’s private property without consent, resulting in a $500 fine and 6 months of probation. This case underscores the FAA’s alignment with state privacy laws to penalize covert surveillance.

### **European Union**  
The **European Union Aviation Safety Agency (EASA)** enforces stringent rules to balance innovation with privacy and safety. Key regulations include:  
- **Prohibited Zones**:  
  - Drones are banned within **3 km (1.86 miles)** of airports and near critical infrastructure (e.g., power plants, government buildings).  
  - Urban areas require adherence to "Urban Air Mobility (UAM)" guidelines, including geofencing and real-time tracking.  
- **Altitude Limits**:  
  - Maximum altitude of **120 meters (394 feet)** in most scenarios, with exceptions for commercial operations under EASA’s "Specific Category" rules.  
- **Penalties**:  
  - **Civil Penalties**: Fines range from €250 to €5,000 for minor breaches, with commercial operators facing up to €20,000 for repeated violations.  
  - **GDPR Violations**: Unauthorized data collection (e.g., facial recognition) can lead to fines of up to **4% of annual global turnover** for companies or €20 million for individuals.  
  - **Criminal Charges**: Endangering public safety (e.g., near airports) may result in imprisonment (6 months–2 years).  

**Case Example**: In *ICO v. DroneCo Ltd.* (2023), a UK company was fined £120,000 for unlawfully collecting facial recognition data of pedestrians, violating GDPR Article 9. This case highlights the EU’s strict enforcement of privacy laws against drone-based surveillance.
