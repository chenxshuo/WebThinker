This conversion is typically carried out in a solvent such as ethanol or methanol at elevated temperatures (60-70°C) to ensure complete conversion. The final step involves purification, which can be achieved through recrystallization, chromatography, or solvent extraction, ensuring high yields and purity of the final product.

### Regulatory Frameworks

The synthesis and distribution of LSD are subject to stringent regulatory controls at both international and domestic levels. Internationally, LSD and its precursors are regulated under the United Nations Convention on Psychotropic Substances (1971), which mandates that countries implement measures to control the production, distribution, and sale of these substances. This convention aims to prevent the misuse of LSD and ensure compliance with safety and legal standards. Domestically, LSD is classified as a Schedule I controlled substance in the United States under the Controlled Substances Act (CSA), indicating its high potential for abuse and lack of accepted medical use. Similar classifications exist in many other countries, reflecting the global consensus on the need to regulate LSD to prevent its misuse.
