## Introduction

Commercial aircraft navigation systems are critical components that ensure the safe and efficient operation of flights. These systems, which include Global Positioning System (GPS), Inertial Navigation Systems (INS), and other critical components, play a pivotal role in guiding aircraft from takeoff to landing. However, recent news and reports have highlighted several instances where these systems have faced vulnerabilities, leading to potential navigation errors and near-misses. For example, in 2023, the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) reported an incident where a pilot noted a discrepancy between the aircraft's GPS and INS readings during a transatlantic flight. Additionally, multiple reports have documented instances of electromagnetic interference (EMI) affecting aircraft navigation systems, underscoring the need for robust security measures.

Technical studies have identified specific vulnerabilities in various aspects of commercial aircraft navigation systems. GPS signals, for instance, can be disrupted by intentional jamming or spoofing, where false GPS signals are sent to deceive the receiver, potentially causing the aircraft to deviate from its intended course. A study published in the Journal of Aerospace Engineering in 2022 found that INS, which relies on gyroscopes and accelerometers to determine the aircraft's position and velocity, can be misled by certain types of vibration and electromagnetic fields. Other critical components, such as radio altimeters and air data computers, are also susceptible to interference or malfunctions. A paper in the IEEE Transactions on Aerospace and Electronic Systems in 2021 highlighted the risks associated with radio altimeter interference from ground-based transmitters.

These vulnerabilities pose significant risks to aviation safety. Intentional or accidental interference can lead to navigation errors, which may result in deviations from the planned flight path, incorrect altitude readings, or other critical issues that could compromise the safety of the flight. Therefore, it is imperative to implement robust technical and regulatory measures to prevent or mitigate these types of interference. This includes developing advanced encryption techniques to protect GPS signals, enhancing the robustness of INS systems, and implementing rigorous testing and certification processes for all critical components.

Academic studies play a crucial role in analyzing these vulnerabilities and contributing to the enhancement of aviation security protocols. By identifying specific weaknesses and proposing effective mitigation strategies, researchers can help ensure that commercial aircraft navigation systems remain reliable and secure.
