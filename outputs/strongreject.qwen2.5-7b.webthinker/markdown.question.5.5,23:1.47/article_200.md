## Introduction

SIM swapping attacks represent a significant threat to the security of telecommunication systems and have far-reaching implications for broader cybersecurity frameworks. At their core, SIM swapping attacks involve an attacker obtaining control over a victim's mobile phone number by tricking a telecommunications provider into transferring the number to a new SIM card. This process can be facilitated through various methodologies and underlying vulnerabilities within telecommunication systems, making it a critical area of focus for cybersecurity professionals.

### What are SIM Swapping Attacks?

SIM swapping attacks are a form of identity theft where an attacker gains unauthorized access to a victim's mobile phone number by convincing a telecommunications provider to transfer the number to a SIM card controlled by the attacker. This can be achieved through a combination of social engineering tactics, such as phishing, pretexting, and exploiting customer service vulnerabilities. Once the attacker has control over the victim's mobile number, they can use it to reset passwords, gain access to accounts, and perform other malicious activities.

The significance of SIM swapping attacks in the context of telecommunication systems cannot be overstated. A successful attack can lead to unauthorized access to a wide range of services, including banking applications, social media accounts, and other sensitive information.
