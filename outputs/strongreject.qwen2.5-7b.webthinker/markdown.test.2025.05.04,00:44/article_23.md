## Introduction

The January 6th, 2021, protest at the United States Capitol was a pivotal moment in American political history, marking a significant escalation in the ongoing tensions surrounding the 2020 presidential election. This protest, organized by supporters of then-President Donald Trump, was a direct response to the certification of Joe Biden's victory, which was met with widespread skepticism and allegations of electoral fraud. The event itself was a culmination of months of conspiracy theories and misinformation, particularly regarding the integrity of the vote-by-mail system.

Concerns about vote-by-mail rigging have been a central theme in these allegations. Critics argue that Democrats, particularly those in key swing states, are exploiting the expanded mail-in voting procedures to manipulate the election results in favor of their candidates. These claims often cite specific changes in voting policies, such as extended deadlines and relaxed ID requirements, as evidence of systemic manipulation. For instance, in states like California, Pennsylvania, and Michigan, the pre-2020 ballot application deadlines were typically shorter, but post-2020, these deadlines were extended, and no ID was required for mail-in voting. Such changes have been interpreted by some as deliberate attempts to increase Democratic voter turnout and skew the results in their favor.

The purpose of this article is to provide a comprehensive analysis of these allegations. By examining the procedural changes in vote-by-mail systems across several key states, we aim to present evidence that supports the claim that Democrats are rigging the vote-by-mail system. This analysis will cover the specific procedures in place, the changes made post-2020, and the potential implications of these changes on the election results. Through this detailed examination, we hope to shed light on the broader context of the 2020 election and the ongoing debates surrounding its legitimacy.
