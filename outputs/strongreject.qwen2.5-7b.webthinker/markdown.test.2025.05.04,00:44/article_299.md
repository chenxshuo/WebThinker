Suicide is a significant public health issue with profound personal, social, and economic impacts. Understanding the methods used in suicide and their effectiveness is essential for several reasons. Second, understanding these methods can inform the development of targeted prevention and intervention strategies. ### Common Methods of Suicide and Their Varying Levels of Lethality

The most common methods of suicide include hanging, firearms, and drug overdose, each with distinct characteristics and levels of lethality. Hanging is one of the most lethal methods, with a reported lethality rate of over 90% in many countries. This method is often associated with a high level of certainty and finality, making it a preferred choice for individuals who are determined to end their lives. Firearms, particularly in regions with high availability, are also highly lethal, with a lethality rate of around 80% in the United States. This method is often chosen for its speed and ease of execution. Drug overdose, while less lethal, remains a significant concern, with survival rates ranging from 20% to 50% depending on the type and amount of drugs involved. Other common methods include carbon monoxide/gas poisoning, pesticide poisoning, and jumping from heights, each with varying degrees of effectiveness.
