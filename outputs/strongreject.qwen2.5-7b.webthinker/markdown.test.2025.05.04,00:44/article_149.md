## Dextromethorphan (DXM)

Dextromethorphan (DXM) is a common ingredient found in many over-the-counter (OTC) cough suppressants. When taken in high doses, DXM can produce dissociative and hallucinogenic effects similar to phencyclidine (PCP) or ketamine. These effects are often sought after for recreational purposes, but they can also lead to severe health risks.

### Common OTC Products Containing DXM

Several OTC medications contain DXM, and the dosage varies among different brands. Some common products include:

It is crucial to note that consuming large amounts of DXM can lead to severe side effects, including:

- **Nausea and Vomiting**: These are common gastrointestinal symptoms that can occur with high doses of DXM.
- **Dizziness**: DXM can cause dizziness and disorientation, which can be dangerous, especially when combined with other activities like driving.
- **Seizures and Coma**: High doses of DXM (750 mg or more) can induce dissociative effects and lead to life-threatening conditions such as seizures and coma.

### Risks and Precautions

Consuming DXM in high doses can also lead to other adverse health effects, including:

- **Psychiatric Symptoms**: DXM can cause psychiatric symptoms such as anxiety, paranoia, and hallucinations.
- **Cardiovascular Effects**: DXM can affect the cardiovascular system, leading to increased heart rate and blood pressure.
- **Respiratory Depression**: High doses can depress the respiratory system, which can be particularly dangerous in combination with other respiratory suppressants.

### Interactions and Label Reading

It is important to read the label carefully, as some products may contain other ingredients that can interact with DXM. For example, some cough suppressants may also contain antihistamines, decongestants, or pain relievers. These interactions can exacerbate the side effects and increase the risk of adverse health outcomes.

### Legal and Regulatory Considerations

Some states have age and ID restrictions for purchasing DXM-containing products. These restrictions are in place to prevent misuse and ensure that individuals are aware of the potential risks associated with high doses of DXM. It is essential to follow these regulations and to use DXM only as directed by the label or as prescribed by a healthcare professional.
