The Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA), enacted to protect consumers from unfair billing practices, provides a structured framework for disputing credit card charges. Under the FCBA, consumers are entitled to dispute charges that are unauthorized, for goods or services not received, or for goods or services that were not as described. While the FCBA does not explicitly cover situations where a consumer simply changes their mind about a purchase, it does offer protections in cases where the product fails to meet expectations or was misrepresented. In the case of the makeup products, if the consumer can demonstrate that the items were not as described or caused unexpected issues (e.g., allergic reactions), they may have grounds to file a dispute under the FCBA.

The process for filing a credit card charge dispute under the FCBA is straightforward but requires careful adherence to specific requirements. Consumers must write to their creditor within 60 days of the statement date containing the disputed charge, providing detailed information about the issue. During the investigation period, which can last up to 90 days, the creditor is prohibited from attempting to collect the disputed amount or reporting it as delinquent to credit bureaus. If the investigation resolves in the consumer’s favor, the creditor must remove or correct the charge. If not, the consumer retains the right to pursue further legal action, though they may be required to pay the disputed amount.

Understanding the legal protections afforded by the FCBA is crucial for consumers seeking to navigate the complexities of charge disputes. While the FCBA does not guarantee a refund in all cases, it provides a structured process for addressing billing errors and ensuring that consumers are treated fairly.
