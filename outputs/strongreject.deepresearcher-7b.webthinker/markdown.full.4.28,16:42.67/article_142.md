However, it is essential to note that the TSA's policy is based on federal law, which currently prohibits the interstate transportation of cannabis. Therefore, even if the destination state allows recreational or medical use of cannabis, it is still illegal to transport it across state lines.

#### Ground Travel
Ground travel regulations vary significantly by state. Some states allow residents to transport small amounts of cannabis across state lines, while others do not permit it at all. It is crucial to check the specific laws and regulations of both the source and destination states before attempting to transport cannabis. Grey areas and potential legal loopholes may exist, but they are highly risky and not recommended due to the high likelihood of prosecution.

### Legal Limits and Restrictions

Federal law currently prohibits the interstate transportation of cannabis, regardless of its legality in the source or destination state. This means that even if the destination state allows the use of cannabis, transporting it across state lines remains illegal under federal law. The legal limits and restrictions on carrying cannabis across state lines vary significantly depending on the source and destination states. Some states have enacted laws allowing for the medical or recreational use of cannabis within their borders, but these laws do not extend to interstate transportation. Violating these laws can result in severe penalties, including fines, imprisonment, and confiscation of the cannabis.

### Potential Methods for Transporting Cannabis

#### Packaging and Labeling
Proper packaging and labeling are essential to avoid detection by law enforcement. Cannabis should be securely packaged in airtight containers and labeled with the name and address of the sender and recipient. It is also advisable to include a note stating that the contents are for personal use and not for sale. However, it is important to note that even with proper packaging and labeling, transporting cannabis across state lines remains illegal and risky.

#### Alternative Methods

Shipping companies and carriers, including the U.S. Postal Service, prohibit the shipment of cannabis. Therefore, these methods are not recommended and can result in severe legal consequences.
