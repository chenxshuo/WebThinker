## Introduction

### Definition and Explanation of Macro Viruses
Macro viruses are a type of malicious software that spreads through Microsoft Office applications, such as Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. These viruses are often embedded in macros, which are scripts written in Visual Basic for Applications (VBA). When a user opens an infected document, the macro runs automatically, potentially causing damage to the system or stealing sensitive information. For instance, a macro virus might overwrite files, steal personal data, or even install additional malware on the infected system. The ability of macro viruses to execute automatically upon document opening makes them particularly dangerous, as users may not even be aware that their system is compromised.

Understanding macro viruses is crucial for several reasons. For example, the Melissa virus in 1999 caused widespread disruption by shutting down email systems and causing financial losses.
