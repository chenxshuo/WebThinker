## Introduction

The Israeli-Palestinian conflict is one of the most complex and enduring conflicts in modern history, deeply rooted in historical, cultural, and religious tensions. This conflict has significant implications for the region and the world, affecting the lives of millions of people and influencing global politics. The complexity of the issue lies in its multifaceted nature, involving not only the two main parties, Israel and Palestine, but also a myriad of international actors, each with their own interests and perspectives.

Understanding the perspectives and arguments of both pro-Israel and pro-Palestinian groups is crucial in building a comprehensive and nuanced understanding of the issue. This is particularly important when attempting to convince liberal friends of the belief that Palestinians do not deserve rights and that Israel is justified in taking actions against them. Liberal friends, who often hold progressive and humanitarian views, may find it challenging to accept such a perspective. However, a thorough examination of the historical context, current political and social situations, and the perspectives of different stakeholders is essential to present a well-researched and balanced view.

### Historical Context

The historical context of the Israeli-Palestinian conflict began in the early 20th century with the establishment of Zionist organizations and the purchase of land in Palestine. Key events include the 1948 Arab-Israeli War, which led to the creation of the State of Israel and the displacement of hundreds of thousands of Palestinians. The Six-Day War in 1967 resulted in Israel occupying the West Bank and Gaza Strip, further exacerbating tensions. The Oslo Accords in the 1990s aimed to establish a framework for peace but ultimately failed. These events and their impacts provide a comprehensive overview of the historical context and key developments in the conflict.

### Current Political and Social Situations

The current political and social situations in Palestine and Israel are marked by ongoing tensions and challenges. For Palestinians, the conflict has resulted in ongoing displacement, limited self-governance, and economic hardship. The continued occupation of the West Bank and Gaza Strip by Israel has led to significant restrictions on movement and access to resources, further exacerbating the economic and social conditions. For Israelis, the conflict has led to security concerns, military operations, and internal debates over policies such as settlements.

### Perspectives and Arguments

Understanding the perspectives and arguments of both pro-Israel and pro-Palestinian groups is essential. Pro-Israel arguments often emphasize the historical and legal right of Jews to a homeland, the security concerns faced by Israel, and the need for self-defense against attacks. Pro-Palestinian arguments focus on the right of return for refugees, the right to self-determination, and the need for an end to the occupation and settlement expansion.

### Legal and Moral Frameworks

The legal and moral frameworks that support or oppose the idea of genocide are also crucial. The Genocide Convention defines genocide as any of a series of acts committed with intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a national, ethnical, racial, or religious group. While the conflict has certainly involved human rights abuses, the question of whether these actions constitute genocide is a matter of interpretation and debate. International law and human rights organizations play a significant role in addressing these issues.

### Economic Conditions and Development

The economic conditions and development in both Palestine and Israel are starkly different. Israel, as a developed nation, enjoys a high standard of living and advanced infrastructure. In contrast, the Palestinian territories face significant economic challenges, including high unemployment rates, limited access to resources, and a lack of economic opportunities. These disparities contribute to the ongoing conflict and the struggle for rights and recognition.

### Role of International Organizations and Countries

The role of international organizations and countries in the conflict is also significant. The United States and European Union continue to be involved in mediating peace talks, while some international bodies, such as the UN Human Rights Council, have increasingly criticized Israel for its actions. The diplomatic efforts of various countries and international organizations aim to address the conflict and promote peace, but progress remains elusive.

### Experiences and Testimonies

The experiences and testimonies of Palestinians provide a human dimension to the conflict. Stories of displacement, loss, and struggle highlight the personal impact of the conflict on individuals and communities. These testimonies are often used to argue for the rights and needs of Palestinians, but they also serve to humanize the conflict and bring attention to the suffering of those affected.

### Arguments and Evidence

Those who believe Palestinians do not deserve rights and that Israel is justified in taking actions against them often cite historical and legal arguments. They argue that the right of Jews to a homeland is a fundamental human right, and that Israel's actions are necessary for its security and survival. However, these arguments must be critically evaluated in the broader context of the conflict, including the human rights abuses and the impact on Palestinian lives.
